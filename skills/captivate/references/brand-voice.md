# Brand Voice Reference

## Voice Principles

- **Professional yet approachable** — write like a trusted expert, not a corporate memo
- **Results-focused** — lead with outcomes, not features or processes
- **Direct and confident** — no hedging, no passive voice, no unnecessary qualifiers
- **Human capability emphasis** — frame AI and automation as tools that enhance people, not replace them
- **No jargon or corporate speak** — if it sounds like a press release, rewrite it

## Approved Terms

Use these freely — they're part of the brand vocabulary:

AI, Revenue, Automation, Go-to-Market (GTM), Prompt Engineering, RevOps, Valuation, Churn, Workflows, Human Capability, Decision-Making, Strategy, Systems, Playbooks, Activate, Amplify, Align, Architect, SQLs, MRR, Pipeline, SDR, AE, BDR, MEDDIC

Action verbs to favor: activate, architect, amplify, build, drive, transform, scale, execute, convert, generate, close, accelerate

## Banned Terms and Phrases

Never use these — they signal vague thinking and erode trust:

**Buzzwords:** hype, leverage, utilize, synergy, holistic, transformative, robust, scalable, cutting-edge, groundbreaking, game-changer, paradigm shift, best practices, innovative, seamless, comprehensive, dynamic, impactful, unprecedented

**Corporate filler:** empower, optimize, streamline, foster, facilitate, enhance, drive (when used abstractly), enable, actionable insights, deep dive, journey, ecosystem, stakeholders, pivotal

**Weak qualifiers:** very, really, quite, somewhat, basically, essentially, generally

**Filler words (remove from transcripts too):** um, uh, like (filler), you know, sort of, kind of

## Copywriting Frameworks

Apply the right framework based on the content goal:

---

### AIDA — Use for awareness and engagement content
**Attention** → Grab with a surprising stat, bold claim, or unexpected angle
**Interest** → Explain why this matters to the reader's specific situation
**Desire** → Show the outcome/transformation they want
**Action** → One clear, low-friction next step

Best for: LinkedIn posts introducing a new idea, YouTube intro scripts, top-of-funnel content

---

### PAS — Use for problem-aware audiences
**Problem** → Name the pain point precisely (be specific, not generic)
**Agitation** → Amplify the cost of not solving it — time, money, missed opportunity
**Solution** → Present the answer clearly and concisely

Best for: LinkedIn posts targeting practitioners, sales-adjacent content, Shorts targeting common frustrations

---

### FAB — Use for showcasing a product, method, or framework
**Feature** → What it is or does
**Advantage** → Why that feature is better than the alternative
**Benefit** → What the reader gains in their real life/work

Best for: Carousel slides, LinkedIn posts about frameworks, infographics

---

### BAB — Use for before/after transformation stories
**Before** → Paint the current, painful state the reader is in
**After** → Describe the world after the problem is solved
**Bridge** → Explain how to get from Before to After

Best for: Case studies, testimonial-based content, YouTube Shorts with transformation angles

---

## Validation Checklist

Run this before delivering any asset:

**Grammar and accuracy**
- [ ] Zero spelling or grammar errors
- [ ] All quotes are verbatim from source — no paraphrasing
- [ ] All statistics include context and are traceable to source material
- [ ] Any unverifiable claims are flagged, not published as fact

**Brand voice**
- [ ] No banned terms or filler words in final copy
- [ ] No passive voice
- [ ] Tone is direct and confident, not hedging
- [ ] Approved terms used where appropriate

**Platform requirements**
- [ ] Word counts within platform limits (see platform-specs.md)
- [ ] Hashtag counts within limits
- [ ] Timestamps in correct format (HH:MM:SS)
- [ ] CTAs are specific and low-friction (not "click here" or "learn more")

**Completeness**
- [ ] All requested asset types generated
- [ ] Each asset includes metadata (source, timestamp, platform, validation status)
- [ ] Manifest file lists all generated assets
- [ ] Critical issues halted for review; non-critical issues noted in validation report
