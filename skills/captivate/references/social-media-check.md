# Kane Callaway — Content Mastery Framework
## Social Media Check Reference

Use this as a pre-writing checklist to evaluate any video concept or social post before committing to a structure. Apply the frameworks here to stress-test hooks, angles, and content type choices.

---

## The Core Philosophy

**Volume negates everything.** Strategy is commoditized once you know it. The real advantage is making one rep, iterating off it, and shortening the distance between iterations. Most people quit at rep 20-30 when they realize it'll take 500 to get good. Winners are egoless about learning and never stop.

---

## The Five Content Games

Identify which game you're playing before anything else. The game determines everything downstream.

**Game 1 — Entertainment Media:** Maximum views at all costs. Monetized via AdSense, TikTok creator fund, brand deals. Mr. Beast, streamers, anyone chasing audience size. Broad topics, sensational, high TAM ideas.

**Game 2 — Education Media:** Same goal (views), but content viewers can actually apply tactically to fix a problem. Anything else is entertainment. A tech/AI channel can look educational but actually be entertainment — if viewers can't walk away and implement something, it's entertainment.

**Game 3 — Commoditized Consumer Products:** Physical products people already understand (couches, clothing, hats). No education needed. Just awareness. Show it looking cool.

**Game 4 — Consumer Products Requiring Education:** Like AG1 — you don't know what a green powder is on sight. Short education required to close the gap before purchase.

**Game 5 — B2B / Services / Coaching / Info Products / SaaS:** Everything requiring significant education and trust-building. This is where most entrepreneurs should be playing.

**The blunt take:** The media game (Games 1 & 2) is arbing to zero. AI content will run that game over. Mr. Beast, Netflix, the NFL are the winners — you will not beat them. For anyone building a business, Games 3, 4, and 5 are where you want to be.

**Rich plays Game 5.** Every video should be Education Media within that game — viewers must be able to implement something after watching.

## The 7 LEGO Brick Framework

The most important tactical framework for content evaluation. Each element is a "brick" — they snap together to build content that stops scrolling and holds attention.

Apply this analysis to any piece of content before writing or approving it.

### Brick 1 — The Hook
Does it stop the scroll in the first 3 seconds?

Evaluate on:
- **Specificity:** "I built an N8N workflow in 10 minutes" beats "AI automation is powerful"
- **Proof implied:** Does the hook imply a result exists?
- **Curiosity gap:** Does the viewer need to watch to get the payoff?
- **Relevance:** Does it speak to the exact person with the exact problem?

Weak hook: "In today's video, we're going to talk about AI automation."
Strong hook: "I just built a complete N8N workflow in under 10 minutes. Here's how."

### Brick 2 — The Angle
What's the unique perspective or approach?

Every topic has been covered before. The angle is what makes this version worth watching. Options:
- Contrarian: "Everyone's doing this wrong"
- Speed: "I did this in [X] time"
- Specificity: "For [specific person] with [specific problem]"
- Result-first: Lead with the outcome, then explain how
- Before/After: Show the transformation, then unpack it

### Brick 3 — The TAM (Total Addressable Market)
Is this a problem enough people have?

Before committing to a topic, ask: How many people have this exact problem? If the answer is "a very specific niche of a niche," the video will underperform regardless of quality. Automation Nation's sweet spot: problems that founders, solopreneurs, and agency owners share — not problems unique to one tool or one workflow.

### Brick 4 — The Information Density
Is every scene adding new value?

Watch for:
- Scenes that restate what was already said
- Transitions that don't advance the narrative
- Examples that illustrate the same point twice
- Setup that goes on longer than the insight

Each scene should add a new brick — new information, new proof, new shift in understanding.

### Brick 5 — The Retention Architecture
Does the structure keep people watching?

Techniques:
- **Open loop:** Introduce a question or promise early, answer it later
- **Pattern interrupt:** Change visual pace, tone, or format every 15-20 seconds
- **Stakes:** Make clear what the viewer loses if they don't keep watching
- **Micro-revelations:** Small "aha" moments throughout, not just at the end

### Brick 6 — The Proof
Is there evidence before explanation?

The order matters: show the result, then explain how you got there. Never explain a process before demonstrating it works. For Automation Nation: show the workflow running before walking through how to build it.

### Brick 7 — The CTA Architecture
Does the call-to-action convert?

Rules:
- One primary CTA, not three
- Warm offer, not a demand: "Go try it free" beats "Subscribe now!"
- Specific: Tell them exactly what to do and why
- Earned: The CTA should feel like the natural next step, not a pivot

For Automation Nation: PromptAnything.io free → Bishop AI → subscribe. Always that order. Always that tone.

## Pre-Write Checklist

Before writing any script or social post, verify:

- [ ] Which Content Game is this? (Should be Game 5 for Rich)
- [ ] Is this Education Media — can viewers implement something after?
- [ ] Does the hook stop the scroll in 3 seconds? Is it specific?
- [ ] What's the angle — why this version, why now?
- [ ] Is the TAM large enough?
- [ ] Does PromptAnything.io appear in the first half?
- [ ] Is proof shown before explanation?
- [ ] Does every scene add a new brick?
- [ ] Is the CTA a warm offer in the right order?
