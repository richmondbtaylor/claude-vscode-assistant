## Target Segments with High Lead Potential

### 1. Mid-Market Companies (100–2,000 employees)
Mid-market businesses are increasingly outpacing enterprises in AI adoption speed, but face key hurdles:
- Limited internal AI/ML talent
- Scattered data and poor governance
- Failed or stalled pilots
- Change management gaps

These companies are actively seeking external consultants with **done-for-you automation** and **team upskilling**, which aligns directly with Bishop AI's offer.

**Recommended outreach angle:** "You've tried AI tools. We make them actually work — and train your team to keep them running."

### 2. Financial Services (Banks, Insurers, Wealth Managers)
FSIs face the same regulatory scrutiny as large enterprises but without dedicated compliance teams. They need AI that:
- Automates manual processes
- Maintains audit trails
- Is explainable and defensible to regulators

### 3. Healthcare & Life Sciences
Complex workflows, large volumes of documentation, and regulatory complexity make healthcare a strong fit for AI automation. Companies are actively hiring for "Healthcare AI Workflow" product roles, signaling internal investment and consulting receptivity.

### 4. Professional Services (Legal, Accounting, HR Firms)
These firms have high labor costs, repetitive document-heavy workflows, and are under competitive pressure to modernize. They are less likely to have internal AI teams, making them strong candidates for outsourced automation.

### 5. Manufacturing & Supply Chain
AI in logistics, inventory, and predictive maintenance is a key 2026 growth area. Mid-market manufacturers are looking for practical, ROI-focused implementations.
