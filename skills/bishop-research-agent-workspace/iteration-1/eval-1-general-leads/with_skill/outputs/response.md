## Research Notes

**Patterns across leads:**
- Creator economy companies are expanding product surface faster than their internal ops can absorb. The pitch angle is consistent: new product lines + fast creator acquisition = workflow debt accumulating in onboarding, payouts, and support.
- B2B SaaS companies at Series A/B are building out RevOps for the first time. The "zero to one" mandate (as seen at Notabene) is a recurring pattern — and is a faster entry point than competing with an existing internal ops team.
- Manual billing, affiliate payout reconciliation, and creator onboarding are the three most common pain points across the creator economy leads.

**Suggested outreach sequence:**
1. Start with Notabene — highest urgency signal (active job posting = budget allocated, problem acknowledged).
2. Stan Store — Gary Vee partnership is current news; reference it to show you're paying attention.
3. Beehiiv — platform expansion is public; tie the pitch directly to their November 2025 announcement.
4. Passes — slightly lower signal visibility but well-funded and operationally stretched.
5. Rocketlane — warm pitch; they understand ops value by definition, but make sure the angle is their internal GTM, not their product.

**Flags:**
- Beehiiv and Stan Store are both LA/NYC-based; if Bishop has regional focus or case studies from either market, lead with that.
- Ramp ($32B valuation, 1,100+ employees) and Rippling ($16.8B, Series G) were disqualified — too large, already have internal AI teams.
- GRIN ($110M Series B, ~$1B valuation) was reviewed but set aside — their core platform is influencer marketing automation itself; harder to pitch AI automation to a company whose product is automation.
