## Research Notes

**Patterns across these leads:**

1. **Post-funding ops gaps are the common thread.** Five of the seven companies here closed funding rounds in 2024–2025. Every one of them is in a scaling sprint where manual ops haven't caught up to growth plans yet. Bishop AI's timing advantage is real — pitch within 3–6 months of a funding announcement.

2. **Influencer ops is a proven angle.** GRIN and Humanz both sit on top of deeply manual workflows that their customers perform. Rather than pitching the platform directly on their internal ops (though that works too), there's a channel play: help them build automation that makes their product stickier for customers. That's a different conversation — more partnership than vendor.

3. **Whop is the highest-conviction lead.** $200M raise, explicit international expansion mandate, publicly lean team, and a founder culture that values automation. The pitch practically writes itself.

4. **Suggested outreach sequence:**
   - Week 1: Whop (highest conviction, clearest pain)
   - Week 1: Humanz (post-acquisition integration is time-sensitive)
   - Week 2: GRIN (platform angle, customer retention framing)
   - Week 2: Beehiiv (ops gap is well-documented, direct ICP fit)
   - Week 3: Passes, Fanbase (Series A/B stage, earlier-stage conversation)
   - Week 4: Kajabi (largest company, longer sales cycle, potential channel partner)

5. **Flag — Kajabi size risk.** At $115M ARR and 400–500 employees, Kajabi is approaching the upper end of Bishop AI's sweet spot. They may already have internal ops tooling or vendor relationships. Approach as a channel partner angle (helping their creator base) rather than a pure direct sale.
