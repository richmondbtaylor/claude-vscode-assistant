# Prospect List: Mid-Market Fintech Companies Scaling Ops Teams
**Prepared for:** Bishop AI
**Date:** March 7, 2026
**Criteria:** Fintech, mid-market (roughly 100–1,000 employees), actively scaling operations teams

---

## Overview

Mid-market fintech companies at Series B through Series D stages are in an active build-out phase for operations talent in 2025–2026. Drivers include post-funding headcount expansion, compliance infrastructure requirements that intensify around the 200-employee mark, cross-border expansion, and a push toward leaner ops through AI tooling. These companies are the most likely buyers for solutions that help them build, manage, or support scaling operations functions.

---

## Prospect List

### 1. Kalshi
- **HQ:** New York, NY
- **Size:** 101–200 employees
- **Stage:** Series C ($185M raised in 2025, $2.0B valuation)
- **Investors:** Sequoia, Y Combinator
- **What they do:** Regulated financial exchange for event contracts and prediction markets
- **Why they're scaling ops:** Post-Series C capital deployment; entering new regulatory jurisdictions; compliance and risk operations expansion required for an exchange model
- **Key ops pain points:** Regulatory compliance at scale, risk operations, trading operations infrastructure
- **Suggested outreach angle:** Exchange operations are uniquely complex — compliance, risk desk, and settlement functions all need to scale together

---

### 2. Current
- **HQ:** New York, NY
- **Size:** 201–500 employees
- **Stage:** Series D ($200M raised in 2024, $2.2B valuation)
- **Investors:** Andreessen Horowitz
- **What they do:** Mobile banking and payments platform
- **Why they're scaling ops:** Post-D funding build-out; expanding product surface area (banking, payments, credit); growing customer base creating operational volume
- **Key ops pain points:** Customer operations at scale, fraud ops, payment operations, KYC/AML workflows
- **Suggested outreach angle:** Consumer fintech at this scale faces customer ops challenges that compound — the team needs both headcount and process infrastructure

---

### 3. Jeeves
- **HQ:** Miami, FL (offices in Mexico City, São Paulo, Bogotá, Toronto, London)
- **Size:** 200–500 employees (estimated)
- **Stage:** Series C ($180M raised)
- **What they do:** Global corporate cards, expense management, and cross-border payments for startups and enterprises
- **Why they're scaling ops:** Multi-country operations; each new market requires local compliance, banking relationships, and ops support
- **Key ops pain points:** Cross-border payment operations, multi-entity compliance, FX operations, customer onboarding across jurisdictions
- **Suggested outreach angle:** Operating across 6+ countries means ops complexity multiplies — each market adds a new compliance and settlement layer

---

### 4. Method Financial
- **HQ:** Austin, TX
- **Size:** 51–200 employees
- **Stage:** Series B ($41.5M raised in January 2025)
- **Investors:** Emergence Capital
- **What they do:** API platform enabling fintechs and lenders to access and manage consumer liability data; real-time debt repayment and financial account connectivity
- **Why they're scaling ops:** Client base grew 250%+ in 12 months; Series B capital earmarked for headcount and product development; VP of Finance & Operations role actively open
- **Key ops pain points:** Scaling ops alongside rapid API usage growth, compliance as a Consumer Reporting Agency (CRA), vendor and partner management
- **Suggested outreach angle:** They have a confirmed ops hiring need and are at the exact inflection point — strong product-market fit with ops infrastructure lagging behind

---

### 5. Nova Credit
- **HQ:** San Francisco, CA
- **Size:** 51–200 employees
- **Stage:** Growth stage (exact round not confirmed, actively hiring)
- **What they do:** Consumer Reporting Agency (CRA) helping lenders use cross-border and alternative credit data for underwriting
- **Why they're scaling ops:** Expanding lender partnerships; CRA status means heavy compliance obligations; data operations and compliance reporting are core
- **Key ops pain points:** Data compliance operations, regulatory reporting, lender onboarding workflows
- **Suggested outreach angle:** CRA-regulated companies have non-negotiable ops requirements — compliance, data governance, and audit trails must scale with the business

---

### 6. Alloy
- **HQ:** New York, NY
- **Size:** 200–500 employees (estimated)
- **Stage:** Growth stage (Series C equivalent)
- **What they do:** End-to-end identity risk management platform for 600+ banks, credit unions, and fintechs
- **Why they're scaling ops:** Rapid customer growth (600+ clients); enterprise sales motion requires customer success and implementation ops; product expansion across fraud, credit, and compliance risk
- **Key ops pain points:** Customer implementation operations, identity verification workflow management, compliance product delivery
- **Suggested outreach angle:** Serving 600 financial institutions means their internal ops has to match enterprise-grade client expectations — scaling the implementation and CS function is critical

---

### 7. Pomelo
- **HQ:** San Francisco, CA
- **Size:** 50–150 employees (estimated)
- **Stage:** Series B ($40M raised in 2024)
- **Investors:** Founders Fund
- **What they do:** Neobank for Latin American immigrants in the US
- **Why they're scaling ops:** Post-funding growth; serving an underbanked population with specific compliance and KYC requirements; cross-border remittance operations
- **Key ops pain points:** KYC/AML at scale, cross-border payment operations, customer support operations for a non-English-primary customer base
- **Suggested outreach angle:** Immigrant financial services comes with layered compliance requirements and high customer ops volume — a lean team needs to punch above its weight

---

### 8. Ripple
- **HQ:** San Francisco, CA
- **Size:** 501–1,000 employees
- **Stage:** Late growth / pre-IPO
- **What they do:** Crypto payment solutions and enterprise blockchain for cross-border payments; operating in 50+ countries
- **Why they're scaling ops:** Expanding into new markets; regulatory environment for crypto payments is evolving rapidly; enterprise customer base requires robust operations
- **Key ops pain points:** Global payment operations, crypto compliance, enterprise customer onboarding, regulatory ops across 50+ jurisdictions
- **Suggested outreach angle:** Operating globally in a regulated crypto environment means ops teams are constantly absorbing new market requirements — complexity scales faster than headcount

---

### 9. 401GO
- **HQ:** Salt Lake City, UT
- **Size:** 50–200 employees (estimated)
- **Stage:** Growth stage (self-described "rapid growth fintech")
- **What they do:** Retirement plan platform for small businesses
- **Why they're scaling ops:** Active VP of Operations hire; growing employer and participant base; ERISA compliance and plan administration are operationally intensive
- **Key ops pain points:** Plan administration operations, ERISA compliance, employer onboarding
- **Suggested outreach angle:** Retirement fintech has an unusual ops profile — regulatory complexity plus high transaction volume at the employer and participant level

---

### 10. Bilt Rewards
- **HQ:** New York, NY
- **Size:** 200–500 employees (estimated)
- **Stage:** Growth round ($150M in August 2024, $3.25B valuation)
- **Investors:** Teachers' Venture Growth
- **What they do:** Loyalty and credit card platform for renters; 4.5M+ homes across rental properties, 1M+ credit card accounts
- **Why they're scaling ops:** Rapid user growth (1M+ cardholders); expanding property partnerships; credit card operations and loyalty program operations both require scaling
- **Key ops pain points:** Credit card operations, loyalty/rewards program operations, property partner management, customer service at scale
- **Suggested outreach angle:** A rewards platform that touches both renters and property managers is operationally complex — two distinct customer segments with different ops needs

## Prioritization Notes

**Highest priority (active ops hiring signal + right-size):**
- Method Financial (confirmed VP of Finance & Operations open role)
- 401GO (confirmed VP of Operations open role)
- Current (post-Series D build-out underway)

**Strong fits (growth stage, clear ops scaling pressure):**
- Kalshi (exchange model = complex ops by nature)
- Jeeves (multi-country ops)
- Nova Credit (CRA compliance obligations)

**Longer-term / larger accounts:**
- Ripple (500-1,000 employees; longer sales cycle)
- Bilt Rewards (consumer fintech, ops at scale)
- Alloy (serves the fintech industry itself — could also be a partner)

## Suggested Next Steps

1. Cross-reference these companies on LinkedIn to identify current ops leadership titles, team sizes, and recent job postings
2. Check Crunchbase or Pitchbook for updated headcount and recent funding announcements to confirm fit
3. Prioritize outreach to Method Financial and 401GO given confirmed open ops roles — they are actively in build mode
