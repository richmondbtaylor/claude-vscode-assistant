# Making Your LinkedIn Graphic On-Brand for Bishop AI

Great — let's walk through every styling decision you need to make so your LinkedIn graphic looks polished, professional, and unmistakably Bishop AI.

---

## 1. Background — Start Here First

The single biggest decision is your background color. Bishop AI is a dark-first brand, so the default recommendation is:

**Deep Navy `#000814`** — This is the most premium, high-authority option. Use it when you want the graphic to feel bold and corporate-tech. This is the right call for most LinkedIn posts where you want to stand out in a scroll.

If you need something lighter (e.g., you're pairing with a white or light-colored screenshot, or the content calls for a "clean" tone):

**Near White `#F9F6F0`** — This is the approved light alternative. It still feels refined rather than stark.

Avoid pure white (`#FFFFFF`) or any background color outside the palette — they'll break the visual identity immediately.

---

## 2. Text Colors — Match to Your Background

Once you've chosen your background, your text color is determined:

| Background | Text Color |
|---|---|
| Deep Navy `#000814` | White `#FAFBFA` or Off-White `#E6E2DE` |
| Dark Navy `#1E2333` | White `#FAFBFA` or Off-White `#E6E2DE` |
| Near White `#F9F6F0` | Deep Navy `#000814` |

The Off-White (`#E6E2DE`) option on dark backgrounds is useful for secondary text — body copy, captions, supporting details — so it reads slightly softer than the headline text.

---

## 3. Accent Colors — Gold First, Blue Second

Bishop AI uses exactly two accent colors. Apply them in this order of priority:

**Gold `#E0B848`** — This is the hero accent. Put it on the most important element: your headline, a key stat, a border or underline, a CTA button, or any element you want the viewer's eye to land on first. Gold signals authority and trust and is what makes Bishop AI graphics recognizable.

**Blue `#1894C9`** — This is the supporting accent. Use it for secondary elements: icons, supporting callouts, a chart element, a decorative shape, or a secondary data point.

Rules to follow:
- Never use both accents on the same element — each should accent a different tier of content.
- Do not introduce any other colors. No purples, greens, reds, or custom brand colors from outside this palette — they will look off immediately.
- Decorative shapes, divider lines, icon fills, and borders should use Gold or Blue only — never the background navies or whites.

---

## 4. Typography — Three Fonts, Used in Hierarchy

Bishop AI uses a strict three-font system. Assign them by content tier:

| Content Type | Font | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Main headline / title | Poppins Bold | Bold (not regular) |
| Subheadings, section labels, callout headers | Montserrat | Regular or SemiBold |
| Body text, bullet points, captions, quotes | Open Sans | Regular or Light |

Critical rules:
- Never mix more than these three fonts in a single graphic.
- Never use Poppins for body text or Open Sans for your headline — the hierarchy matters.
- If any of these fonts are unavailable in your design tool, use the fallbacks: Arial Bold for headings, Helvetica for subheadings, Georgia for body.

---

## 5. Composition and Layout Guidance

**Hierarchy before decoration** — Let your typography create visual structure. A strong Poppins headline in White with a Gold underline on a Deep Navy background is already striking without any additional decoration.

**Less is more** — The Bishop AI brand is intentionally minimal. Resist the urge to add extra colors, competing gradients, or decorative elements that fight the message. One or two accent touches are enough.

**Gold on the primary element** — Whatever you most want the viewer to read or act on, that is where Gold goes.

**Avoid gradients that fight the palette** — Keep any gradients within the approved colors (e.g., Deep Navy to Dark Navy).

---

## 6. Quick Reference Checklist

Before you finalize your LinkedIn graphic, confirm:

- [ ] Background is one of: `#000814`, `#1E2333`, `#E6E2DE`, `#F9F6F0`, or `#FAFBFA`
- [ ] Text color matches the background (white/off-white on dark; deep navy on light)
- [ ] Primary headline uses Poppins Bold
- [ ] Subheadings or section labels use Montserrat
- [ ] Body text or captions use Open Sans
- [ ] Gold `#E0B848` is on the most important accent element
- [ ] Blue `#1894C9` is used only for secondary accent elements, if needed
- [ ] No colors from outside the palette appear anywhere
- [ ] No more than three font families appear on the graphic

---

## 7. Example: Stat-Callout LinkedIn Graphic

Say you're making a stat-callout graphic (e.g., "87% of procurement teams are switching to AI-assisted sourcing"):

- **Background:** Deep Navy `#000814`
- **Headline stat ("87%"):** Poppins Bold, Gold `#E0B848`, large
- **Headline sentence:** Poppins Bold, White `#FAFBFA`, slightly smaller
- **Supporting subtext:** Open Sans Light, Off-White `#E6E2DE`, small
- **Decorative element (horizontal rule or border):** Gold `#E0B848`
- **Optional icon:** Blue `#1894C9`

That combination follows all brand rules and will look premium, dark, and distinctly Bishop AI.
