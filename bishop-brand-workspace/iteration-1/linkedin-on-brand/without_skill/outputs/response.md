## What I Know About Bishop AI

Bishop AI appears to be an AI-focused company, likely operating in the enterprise or B2B space. Companies in this category typically adopt a visual identity that signals technical sophistication, trustworthiness, and modernity.

---

## Styling Recommendations

### 1. Color Palette

For a company named "Bishop AI," the most likely brand palette would lean toward:

- **Primary:** A deep navy or charcoal (`#1A2B4A` range) — conveys authority and intelligence
- **Accent:** A bright electric blue or cyan (`#3B82F6` or `#00C2FF` range) — signals AI/tech innovation
- **Neutral Background:** Off-white or very light gray (`#F8F9FA` or `#FFFFFF`) — keeps it clean and readable
- **Text:** Near-black (`#111827`) for primary copy, mid-gray (`#6B7280`) for secondary

**LinkedIn-specific tip:** Avoid pure white backgrounds — they can disappear against LinkedIn's own white UI.

### 2. Typography

- **Headlines:** A geometric sans-serif — think Inter, DM Sans, or Montserrat. Bold weight (700) for impact.
- **Body / Supporting text:** The same typeface at regular weight (400), or a complementary humanist sans-serif like Source Sans Pro.

**Size hierarchy for a LinkedIn graphic (1200x627px standard):**
- Main headline: 48–64px
- Subheadline or tagline: 24–32px
- Supporting detail / CTA: 16–18px

### 3. Layout & Composition

- **Alignment:** Left-aligned text is easier to read and feels more editorial/professional.
- **White space:** Generous — do not crowd the graphic.
- **Grid:** Use a consistent margin — 60–80px from all edges.
- **Visual hierarchy:** One dominant element (the headline or a key statistic), one supporting element, and a logo lockup. Three layers maximum.

### 4. Logo Placement

- Place the Bishop AI logo in a corner — bottom-right or bottom-left is standard for LinkedIn graphics.
- Use the logo version (light or dark) that contrasts properly with your background color.

### 5. Imagery & Iconography

- If using icons: geometric, line-style icons in the brand accent color.
- If using photography: dark, high-contrast images with a color overlay in the brand palette work well.
