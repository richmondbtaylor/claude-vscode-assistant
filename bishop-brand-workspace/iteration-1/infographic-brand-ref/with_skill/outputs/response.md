# Bishop AI Brand Reference: Infographic for Top 5 Client Results

Here are the exact colors and fonts to use for your infographic, pulled directly from the official Bishop AI brand guidelines.

---

## Color Palette

### Background Colors

| Name | Hex | When to Use |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Deep Navy | `#000814` | Primary/default background -- premium, dramatic feel |
| Dark Navy | `#1E2333` | Secondary background, card surfaces, section panels |

**Recommendation for this infographic:** Use Deep Navy (`#000814`) as the main background. It gives a premium, authoritative look -- ideal for showcasing client results.

---

### Text Colors

| Name | Hex | When to Use |
|------|-----|-------------|
| White | `#FAFBFA` | Primary text on dark backgrounds |
| Off-White | `#E6E2DE` | Secondary text, captions, supporting copy on dark |

**Rule:** Because your background is dark (Deep Navy), all text should be White (`#FAFBFA`) or Off-White (`#E6E2DE`). Never use dark text on a dark background.

---

### Accent Colors

| Name | Hex | When to Use |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Gold | `#E0B848` | PRIMARY accent -- stat callouts, result numbers, section borders, headlines |
| Blue | `#1894C9` | SECONDARY accent -- icons, chart bars, supporting callouts, dividers |

**Rule:** Gold is the hero accent -- put it on your most important numbers and highlights (e.g., "300% ROI", "2x faster"). Use Blue for supporting data elements, icons, or secondary labels.

## Typography

| Role | Font Name | Fallback | Used For |
|------|-----------|----------|---------|
| H1 / H2 Headings | **Poppins Bold** | Arial Bold | Infographic title, client names (largest text) |
| Subheadings / Labels | **Montserrat** | Helvetica | Section labels, result category headers, stat labels |
| Body / Captions | **Open Sans** | Georgia | Descriptions, supporting copy, fine print, footnotes |

**Hierarchy rule:** Poppins Bold at the top (biggest, most prominent), Montserrat in the middle (section and callout labels), Open Sans at the bottom (body and detail text). Do not introduce any other font families.

---

## How to Apply This to Your Infographic

Follow this order when setting up your design tool:

1. **Set the background** to Deep Navy (`#000814`) -- establishes the premium, dark-first identity.
2. **Set all text** to White (`#FAFBFA`) by default; use Off-White (`#E6E2DE`) for captions or secondary labels.
3. **Apply Gold (`#E0B848`)** to your most important elements: the key result numbers, stat callouts, borders on featured cards, and the infographic title or headline.
4. **Apply Blue (`#1894C9`)** to supporting visual elements: icons, chart bars, secondary callout frames, or dividers between client sections.
5. **Set your font hierarchy:** Poppins Bold for the infographic title and any large result numbers; Montserrat for client name labels and result category headers; Open Sans for any descriptive text or supporting copy.
6. **Do not add any other colors** -- the palette is intentionally minimal. Let contrast and typography create visual hierarchy rather than introducing new hues.
